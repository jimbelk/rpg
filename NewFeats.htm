New Feats

This page includes all of the new feats we are using for this campaign.  The item creation feats listed here replace all of the usual Pathfinder item creation feats.

Feat Name Prerequisites Benefit
Craft Magic Item Caster level 1st Gain an increasing pool of craft points that can be used to craft magic items.
Craft Psionic Item Manifester level 1st Gain an increasing pool of craft points that can be used to craft psionic items
Extra Crafting Craft Magic Item or Craft Psionic Item Gain extra craft points.
Legacy Weapon Must own a legacy weapon The power of your legacy weapon increases automatically as you gain levels.

Prerequisite: Caster level 1st.

Initial Craft Points: The number of craft points you gain upon taking the Craft Magic Item feat.

Additional Craft Points: The number of additional craft points you get upon gaining the indicated level.

For example, if you take this feat at 9th level, you immediately gain 11,500 craft points. Furthermore, you will gain an additional 5,000 craft points when you advance to 10th level, an additional 7,100 craft points when you advance to 11th level, and so forth.

To craft a magic item, you must spend a number of craft points equal to the base price of the item. No special materials are required, though you must supply the mundane form of the item to be enchanted (such as a wooden baton for a wand of fireballs, or a well-made cloak for a cloak of resistance). In the case of magic arms or armor, the item to be enchanted must be at least masterwork in quality. Crafting a magic item takes 2 hours if the base price is less than 250 gp, and otherwise takes 1 day for each 1,000 gp in its base price.

At the DM’s discretion, a character may spend the time to craft an item before gaining the requisite craft points, finishing the item “instantly” when the necessary craft points become available.  For instance, Kyra may have been tinkering with Amiri’s sword for a while, but only completes the project when she advances to 6th level and gains the required 2,000 craft points.

When attempting to satisfy the prerequisites for an item you wish to craft, you may use the Craft Magic Item feat in place of one or more of the following feats: Scribe Scroll, Brew Potion, Craft Wand, Craft Staff, Craft Rod, Forge Ring, Craft Magic Arms and Armor, and Craft Wondrous Item. In particular, you can use this feat to craft any of the following items:

  • A scroll of any spell that you know.
  • A wand of any 4th-level or lower spell that you know.
  • A potion of any 3rd-level or lower spell that you know and that targets one or more creatures or objects.
  • Magic armor, shields, and magic weapons, including those found in Ultimate Equipment and other "loose core" sources.
  • Magic rings, rods, and staves.
  • Magic wondrous items.

See the magic item creation rules for further information.  If multiple characters cooperate to craft an item, they may each contribute any number of craft points, as long as the total number of craft points spent is equal to the base price of the item.

Special: When creating a new character above 1st level, selecting this feat always yields initial craft points corresponding to the character’s current level, regardless of when they might have taken the feat. For example, a new 8th-level character with Craft Magic Item always begins play with 8,250 craft points. The character may spend some or all of these craft points during character creation to craft additional starting gear.

Craft Psionic Item [Item Creation]

You can create psionic items.

Prerequisite: Manifester level 1st.

Benefit: This is similar to the Craft Magic Item feat, except that it allows you to craft psionic items such as dorjes, power stones, psicrowns, and so forth. At the DM’s discretion, a psionic character may also craft psionic versions of normal magic items, substituting appropriate powers for the prerequisite spells.

Extra Crafting [Item Creation]

You can create additional magic items.

Prerequisite: Craft Magic Item or Craft Psionic Item

Benefit: You immediately gain half as many craft points as would be granted to a character of your level by the Craft Magic Item feat. In addition, each time you gain a level, you gain half again as many additional craft points as you would from Craft Magic Item alone.

For example, if you take this feat at 12th level, you immediately gain 13,500 craft points (half of 27,000). When you reach 13th level, you will gain an additional 16,500 craft points (11,000 from Craft Magic Item, and an extra 5,500 from this feat).

Special: You can take this feat multiple times. Its effects stack.

Legacy Weapon

You have a weapon of legacy whose power grows as you gain levels.

Prerequisite: You must own a legacy weapon.

Benefit: Choose one legacy weapon that you own.  This can be a famous weapon of legend, or an heirloom weapon that has been passed down in your family or within an organization such as a church that you belong to.  This weapon must be magical, but cannot have more than a +1 enhancement, nor can it have any special abilities such as flaming equivalent to an enhancement bonus.

Starting now, the power of this weapon increases automatically with your level according to the following table.

Level Weapon Power
7th +2
10th +3
12th +4
14th +5
15th +6
16th +7
17th +8
18th +9
19th +10

Here "weapon power" refers to the total enhancement bonus of the weapon, including regular enhancement bonuses as well as enhancements such as flaming or holy chosen by you (see below).

Whenever the power of your legacy weapon increases by one, you may choose to increase the enhancement bonus of the weapon by one (to a maximum of +5), or you may choose a +1 special ability to add to the weapon.  Alternatively, you may add a more powerful special ability in exchange for decreasing the enhancement bonus of your weapon (to a minimum of +1).  For example, you may add a +2 special ability to a weapon in exchange for decreasing its enhancement bonus by one, or a +3 special ability in exchange for decreasing its enhancement bonus by two.  Once a special abilitiy has been added to a legacy weapon, it cannot be removed or changed.

The item creation process cannot be used to increase the enhancement bonus of a legacy weapon, nor can they be used to add special abilities equivalent in cost to an enhancement bonus.  However, the usual item creation process can be used to add enhancements to a legacy weapon that have a fixed cost, such as the impervious property (which costs +3,000 gp).

The extra enhancements provided by this feat only function when you are wielding the legacy weapon yourself.  In this case, the weapon has an effective caster level equal to your character level.
