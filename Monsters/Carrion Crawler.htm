Carrion Crawler

The stink of rotten meat surrounds this multilegged creature with a segmented, 10-foot-long body. Eight writhing tentacles protrude from its head, growing directly from below its clacking mandibles and tooth-filled maw.

SPECIAL ABILITIES

Paralysis (Ex)

Those hit by a carrion crawler’s tentacle attack must succeed at a DC 17 Fortitude save or be paralyzed for 2d4 rounds. (This duration is increased by 1d4 rounds for each additional failed save.) The save DC is Constitution-based.

ECOLOGY

Environment underground

Organization solitary, pair, or cluster (3–5)

Treasure none

DESCRIPTION

Carrion crawlers are aggressive subterranean scavengers, greatly feared for their paralyzing attacks. They scour their underground territory for dead and decaying flesh but won’t hesitate to attack and kill living creatures. Each of a carrion crawler’s tentacles is about 2 feet long and secretes a sticky, paralyzing substance. Like so many hybrid monsters, the carrion crawler may well be the result of arcane experimentation. A carrion crawler weighs about 500 pounds.

Carrion crawlers use their senses of sight and smell to detect carcasses and potential prey. When attacking, a crawler lashes out with its tentacles and tries to paralyze its victim. The tentacles deal no other damage. The creature then kills the paralyzed victim with its bite and devours the flesh. Multiple crawlers do not fight in concert, but each paralyzes as many opponents as possible. The unintelligent creature continues to attack as long as it faces any moving opponents.
