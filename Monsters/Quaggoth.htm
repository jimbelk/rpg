Once per day, a quaggoth can fly into a screaming blood frenzy. This ability functions like an unchained barbarian’s rage, except that the quaggoth suffers no ill effects after the rage ends. The statistics above reflect a raging quaggoth (with changes highlighted in red).

DESCRIPTION

Quaggoths are the evil underdogs of the world. Their ferocity and their 7-foot-tall physiques make them dangerous opponents, but they are no match for the magical wiles of the drow, illithids, and other underground civilizations. Roughly half the quaggoth population is free; the other half is enslaved by one or another underground menace.

Thanks to quaggoths’ beastlike features and their barking, snarling dialect of Undercommon, barely distinguishable as a language, adventurers often call them “bear-men.” Their fur is naturally white, but many quaggoths dye or paint their fur to camouflage themselves better in the underground. Quaggoths prefer eating prey raw, if possible while it is still alive or dying.

Though they may stalk prey and attempt to set ambushes, quaggoths rely on headlong charges, ferocity, and grappling attacks in battle. Roughly half of quaggoths in a typical group use primitive weapons such as clubs, while the other half attack with their natural weapons. Though they are determined combatants, they will flee if more than half their number are killed.
