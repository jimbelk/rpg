Durzagon

A durzagon is the result of secret crossbreeding between a devil and an unsuspecting duergar. From time to time, powerful devils disguised as gray dwarves infiltrate duergar society, seeking out the best mates to bear durzagon children. Most duergar are unaware that they have attracted the attention of diabolical forces. The birth of a half-fiend durzagon is cause for much celebration in the infernal realms.

A durzagon is slightly taller and leaner than a gray dwarf, usually reaching a height of about 5 feet. The half-fiend is bald but sports a wiry, gray beard tinged with red streaks. Its rust-colored skin radiates a palpable heat, and its fingers end in wickedly sharp claws.

Though durzagons relish physical combat, they usually prefer to use spell-like abilities from behind the protection of duergar guards. In melee, durzagons show no fear; they would rather fight to the death than submit to an enemy.

Durzagons almost always hold leadership positions in duergar society, and a large duergar city may have several durzagon residents. The gray dwarves revere these creatures as powerful leaders.

Racial Traits

  • Ability Score Racial Traits: Durzagons are superior to normal duergar in almost every way. They gain +4 Strength, +4 Dexterity, +4 Constitution, +4 Intelligence, +4 Wisdom, and –2 Charisma.
  • Type: Durzagons are outsiders with the native subtype
  • Size: Durzagons are Medium creatures and thus have no bonuses or penalties due to their size.
  • Base Speed: Durzagons have a base speed of 30 feet.
  • Languages: Durzagons begin play speaking Undercommon, Dwarven, and Infernal. Durzagons with high Intelligence scores can choose from the following: Abyssal, Aklo, Draconic, Giant, Goblin, Orc, and Terran. See the Linguistics skill page for more information about these languages.

Defense Racial Traits

  • Durzagon Immunities: Durzagons are immune to paralysis, phantasms, and poison. They gain a +2 racial bonus on saves against spells and spell-like abilities.
  • Energy Resistance: Durzagons have acid resistance 10, cold resistance 10, electricity resistance 10, and fire resistance 10.
  • Damage Reduction: Durzagons have DR 5/magic.  This improves to DR 10/magic at 12th level.
  • Spell Resistance: Durzagons possess spell resistance (SR) equal to 11 plus their total number of class levels.
  • Stability: Durzagons receive a +4 racial bonus to their CMD against bull rush or trip attempts while on solid ground.
  • Natural Armor: A durgazon receives a +1 natural armor bonus.

Offense Racial Traits

  • Natural Weapons: A durzagon has 2 claw attacks that deal 1d6 points of damage, and a secondary bite attack that deals 1d6 points of damage. A durzagon also has a secondary beard attack that deals 1d3 points of damage plus poison.
  • Rend (Ex): If a durzagon hits a foe with both claw attacks, it automatically hits with its beard as well. 
  • Poison (Su): Beard—injury; save Fort DC 14; frequency 1/round for 6 rounds; effect 1d3 Str damage; cure 2 consecutive saves. The save DC is Constitution-based.
  • Smite Good (Su): Once per day, as a swift action, a durzagon can smite good as the smite evil ability of a paladin of the same level as the durzagon, except affecting a good target. The smite persists until the target is dead or the durzagon rests.

Magical Racial Traits

  • Psi-Like Abilities (Psi): A durzagon can use expansion and invisibility each once per day, using its character level as its manifester level (minimum 3rd) and affecting itself only.
  • Spell-Like Abilities (Sp): A durzagon receives additional spell-like abilities depending on its level, using its character level as its caster level. These abilities are cumulative, e.g. a 3rd-level durzagon receives both the darkness and desecrate abilities.  Unless otherwise noted, an ability is usable 1/day.  The save DC's are Intelligence-based.

Senses Racial Traits

  • Superior Darkvision: Durzagons have superior darkvision, allowing them to see perfectly in the dark up to 120 feet.

Weakness Racial Traits

  • Light Blindness: As deep underground dwellers naturally, durzagons suffer from light blindness. Abrupt exposure to any bright light blinds a durzagon for 1 round. On subsequent rounds, they are dazzled as long as they remain in the affected area.
