SPECIAL ABILITIES

Eye Rays (Su)

Each of a beholder’s small eyes can produce a magical ray once per round. On its turn, a beholder can shoot three of these rays (randomly determined) as a free action. The beholder knows which rays are available in a given turn, and can choose its target or targets accordingly.

In addition, three times per round, the beholder can shoot a single randomly determined eye ray at the end of another creature's turn.

Each eye ray has a range of 150 feet and a save DC of 21. The save DCs are Charisma-based. The ten eye rays include:

  • 1. Charm Ray: The target must make a Will save or be charmed for two weeks, as the charm monster spell. During combat, the beholder generally instructs a charmed target to either restrain a comrade or stand aside.
  • 2. Death Ray: The target must make a Will save or take 130 points of damage.  A successful save reduces the damage to 3d6+13. This is a death effect.
  • 3. Disintegration Ray: The target must make a Fortitude save or take 26d6 points of damage, as the disintegrate spell (CL 13th).  A successful save reduces the damage to 5d6.  A target reduced to 0 hp in this fashion is disintegrated.
  • 4. Enervation Ray: The target takes 6d6 points of negative energy damage (no save).
  • 5. Fear Ray: The target must succeed at a Will save or become panicked for 1 minute.  If the Will save succeeds, the target is shaken for 1 round.
  • 6. Paralyzation Ray: The target of this ray must make a Will save or be subject to the effects of a hold person spell for 1 minute.  As with hold person, the target can make a new save each round to end the effect.
  • 7. Petrification Ray: The target must make a Fortitude save or be petrified, as the flesh to stone spell.
  • 8. Sleep Ray: The target must make a Will save or fall alseep. Slapping or wounding awakens an affected creature, but normal noise does not. Awakening a sleeping creature is a standard action. A subject who is not otherwise disturbed wakes up naturally after several hours.
  • 9. Slowing Ray: The target must make a Will save or become staggered  for 1 minute.
  • 10. Telekinesis: A beholder can use this ray to move any unattended object that weighs up to 325 pounds at a speed of up to 20 feet per round.  Alternatively, when used against a creature, this ray allows the beholder to attempt a single combat maneuver (such as a bull rush, trip, or disarm) against the target with a +15 CMB, as with the telekinesis spell (CL 13th).

Antimagic Cone (Su)

A beholder’s central eye continually produces a 150-foot cone of antimagic. This functions just like antimagic field (CL 13th). All magical and supernatural powers and effects within the cone are suppressed—even the beholder’s own eye rays. Once each round, during its turn, the beholder decides whether the antimagic cone is active or not (the beholder deactivates the cone by shutting its central eye).

While the antimagic cone is active, the beholder can change its direction as a free action during its turn, or as an immediate action otherwise. The beholder will usually use this ability to turn and face any enemy who begins casting, ruining the spell.

Flight (Ex)

A beholder’s body is naturally buoyant. This buoyancy allows it to fly at a speed of 20 feet. This buoyancy also grants it a permanent feather fall effect with personal range.

TACTICS

During Combat Beholders often attack without provocation. Though not powerful physically, they often plow right into groups of opponents to use as many of their eyes as they can. When closing with an enemy, a beholder tries to cause as much disruption and confusion as possible.

ECOLOGY

Environment cold hills or underground

Organization solitary, pair, or cluster (3–6)

Treasure double standard

Beholders are the stuff of nightmares. These creatures, also called the “spheres of many eyes” or “eye tyrants,” are deadly adversaries.

A beholder is an 4-foot-wide orb dominated by a central eye and a large, toothy maw. Ten smaller eyes on stalks sprout from the top of the orb.

Beholders are hateful, aggressive, and avaricious, attacking or dominating others whenever they can get away with it. They exhibit a xenophobic intolerance, hating all creatures not like themselves. The basic body type comprises a great variety of beholder subspecies. Some are covered with overlapping chitinous plates; some have smooth hides or snakelike eye tentacles; some have crustacean joints. But even a difference as small as hide color or size of the central eye can make two groups of beholders sworn enemies. Every beholder declares its own unique form to be the “true ideal of beholderhood,” the others being nothing but ugly replicas fit only to be eliminated.

Beholders usually carve out underground lairs for themselves using their disintegrate rays. Beholder architecture emphasizes the vertical, and a lair usually has a number of parallel tubes, with chambers stacked on top of each other. Beholders prefer inaccessible locations that earthbound foes can reach only with difficulty.
