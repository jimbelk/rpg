SPECIAL ABILITIES

Trick Attack (Ex), Debilitating Trick (Ex), Staggering Trick (Ex)

Clyde can trick or startle a foe and then attack when she drops her guard. As a full action, he can move up to his speed. (If he chooses the target of his trick attack before he moves, then his movement does not provoke attacks of opportunity from that target.) Whether or not he moved, he can then make an attack. Just before making his attack, he attempts a Bluff, Intimidate, or Stealth check (with a +1 bonus if a Stealth check).

Trick Attack DC = 20 + target's CR

If he succeeds at the check, the target is flat-footed against the attack, suffering a −2 penalty to AC (see FAQ). If the attack succeeds, he deals 4d8 additional damage and the target suffers one of the following conditions (Clyde's choice):

Flat-footed: The target takes a −2 penalty to AC and cannot take reactions until the beginning of Clyde's next turn.

Off-target: The target takes a −2 penalty to attack rolls until the beginning of Clyde's next turn.

Staggered: The target must make a DC 17 Fortitude save or be staggered until the beginning of Clyde's next turn. A staggered creature can take a single move action or standard action each round, but not both, nor can it take full actions. It can still take swift actions, but not reactions. Once Clyde has used this ability to attempt to stagger a creature, that creature is immune to Clyde's staggering shot for 24 hours.

Clyde can only use this ability with a melee weapon that has the operative special property or with any small arm. Clyde can’t use this ability with a weapon that has the unwieldy special property or that requires a full action to make a single attack.

Note: The extra damage from a trick attack is doubled on a critical hit. See here.

Holographic Clone (Ex)

Clyde can create holographic duplicates of himself that conceal his true location. Twice per day as a standard action, he can create 1d4 images of himself that last for 7 minutes.

This ability otherwise functions as mirror image.  In particular, these images remain in Clyde's space and move with him, mimicking his movements, sounds, and actions exactly. Whenever Clyde is attacked or is the target of a spell that requires an attack roll, the attack has the following result:

Attack hits: Roll randomly to see whether the selected target is real or a hologram. If it is a hologram, the hologram is destroyed.

Attack misses by 5 or less: One of the holograms is destroyed by the near miss.

Attack misses due to a miss chance: Also destroys an image.

Area spells and effects that don’t require an attack roll affect Clyde normally and don’t destroy any of his holograms. Spells with a range of touch are harmlessly discharged if used to destroy a hologram.

An attacker must be able to see the holograms to be fooled. If Clyde is invisible or the attacker is blind, the holograms have no effect. Blindsense doesn’t help distinguish the holograms from the real Clyde, but blindsight is sufficient to do so.

Cloaking Field (Ex)

Clyde can bend light around himelf and muffle any minor sounds he makes, allowing him to nearly vanish when not moving. Even when he moves, he appears only as an outline with blurry features. This cloaking field doesn’t make him invisible, but it does make it easier for him to sneak around. Activating the cloaking field is a move action. While the cloaking field is active, Clyde can use Stealth to hide, even while being directly observed and with no place to hide. Attacking doesn’t end the cloaking field, but it does end that particular attempt to hide. If he remains perfectly still for at least 1 round, he gains a +10 bonus to Stealth checks (which doesn’t stack with invisibility) until he moves.

Clyde's cloaking field lasts for up to 10 rounds before it becomes inactive. While inactive, the cloaking field recharges automatically at the rate of 1 round of cloaking per minute.
