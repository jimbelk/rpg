Important Gods

St. Cuthbert

St. Cuthbert, also known as the Blind God, the Maimed God, or the Hand of Justice, is a lawful good deity of law and justice.  His dogma concerns the protection of the innocent, punishment of wrong-doers, and the preservation of good in the world.

The cult of St. Cuthbert is a secret organization known as the Unseen. Historically, members of the Unseen have been instrumental in organizing the duergar, drow, and svirfneblin to fight against the mind flayers and aboleths, as well as extraplanar threats including demons, devils, and creatures from the far realm. More recently the Unseen have maintained their vigilance against these threats while simultaneously working to stabilize the political situation and combat more mundane threats such as the gibberlings and the derro.

Lolth

Lolth, also known as the Spider Queen or the Dark Mother, is a chaotic evil deity of spiders and drow. She is the most powerful deity of the drow pantheon and demands absolute loyalty from her followers.  Drow society is domainated by the priestesses of Lolth, who fight each other for her favor, and drow culture is entirely consumed by their worship of the goddess.

Lolth thrives on death, destruction, and torture.  She is not loved by the drow—they fear her power, and live their lives in terror of displeasing her. All drow are taught to fear and praise Lolth from a young age, and all families are expected to produce at least one cleric for the goddess.  Lolth teaches that the strong shall survive while the soft and weak shall perish, and she encourages the drow to cull the weak as well as those not wholly dedicated to her. Lolth has a special fondness for spiders, and harming a spider is punishable by death in drow society.


Laduguer

Laduguer, also known as the Gray Protector, is the lawful evil duergar god of domination, hardship, servitiude, and toil. His dogma concerns strict obedience to superiors, empowerment through the exploitation of slaves, and enrichment through mining, craftsmanship, and conquest.

Temples of Laduguer are grim, smoke-filled halls constructed from solid rock.  They are often decorated with weapons and armor and double as forges. Non-duergar are not allowed in the temples, although clerics are willing to provide spellcasting services to non-dwarves in front of a temple for reasonable prices. Laduguer views worship and praise as useless luxuries, and his clerics are expected to spend most of their time working as craftsman, running businesses, or otherwise enriching themselves and increasing the power of the duergar.

 

Y'Chak

Y'Chak, also known as the Violet Flame, is not actually a god, but a different kind of being sometimes called an outer god or elder evil. Aboleths believe that the elder evils are older than the gods—older perhaps than the universe itself—and will continue to exist long after the universe has come to an end. The elder evils are alien and unknowable, operating with motives that mortals can't possibly fathom.

Unlike most elder evils, Y'Chak active communicates with mortals through terrible nightmares and premonitions.  He is worshipped by the aboleths, who regard Y'Chak as the personification of entropy.  According to the aboleths, Y'Chak seeks to move all life towards destruction, madness, and ruin.

The mind flayers seem to believe that Y'Chak was somehow involved in the massive invasion of the world by far realm creatures nearly four centuries ago. Due to subtle manipulation by the aboleths, the mind flayers mistakenly opened a portal to Y'Chak's domain, leading to a huge influx of bizarre aberrations such as cloakers, gibbering mouthers, and gugs. This ended the Awakening War between the mind flayers and their former slaves as all parties joined together to fight the aberrations.

 

Yog-Sothoth

Yog-Sothoth, also known as the Eater of Souls, is a completely alien elder evil who lives in the space between the stars, or perhaps beyond them altogether. The aboleths view Yog-Sothoth as the personification of the void—the nothingness from which the universe sprung and to which it will one day return.  According to the aboleths, Yog-Sothoth seeks the dissolution of reality itself and the negation of all existence.

The cult of Yog-Sothoth works to hasten the world's demise, which they believe they can accomplish by contacting their outer god.  The nature and organization of the cult are mysterious, but dark whispers tell of a Triad of powerful spellcasters who maintain a hidden fortress on the Astral plane. The cult is opposed by the Unseen, who through repeated attacks have weakened the cult and ruined their designs.
