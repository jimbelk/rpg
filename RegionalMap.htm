Map of the North

Here is a map of the local region, known as "The North" because the largest drow cities lie to the south of this map. To give a sense of scale, the Darklake is about 100 miles across.  The colors of the dots show depth beneath the surface:

Red dots show locations in the Upperworld.

Yellow dots show locations in the Middleworld.

Blue dots show locations in the Lowerworld.

Hover over a dot or area to see a description. (The same descriptions appear below the map.)

This map shows the following locations.

Auraumycos: A huge fungal growth in the Upperworld. The whole area is filled with puffballs and fungal creatures, including many myconid villages. Scholars have suggested that Auraumycos is actually a single living creature of enormous size, though its sentience is debated.

Blingdenstone: This ruined svirfneblin city was destroyed roughly 10 years ago by drow from Menzoberranzan. Many of the demons and spider-creatures summoned by the drow still inhabit the area.

Chaulssin: A city ruin from before the Awakening that overhangs a tremendous abyss through which cold wind perpetually screams. The ruins lies on a great spar of stone that juts out into the emptiness, and its ramparts and galleries overlook a terrifying void. The ruin seems to lie partly in the Plane of Shadow, and deadly creatures from the Shadow haunt its streets.

Ch'chitl: A mind flayer stronghold in the Lowerworld, under the southeast portion of the Labyrinth.

Ched Nasad: The City of Shimmering Webs, a large drow city in the Middleworld.  The entire city is built upon huge filaments of calcified spider webbing.

The Darklake: A huge underground lake with different sections connected by a series of locks. The Darklake is so-named because magic light seems unable to penetrate the water farther than 10 feet. The Darklake is a major route for trade and is also home to scattered settlements of kuo-toa. (The party was attacked by Ixzan raiders with an elasmosaurus while crossing the Darklake on the way to Gracklstugh.)

Dolblunde: These are the ruins of the svirfneblin city of Dolblunde. Its location was discovered in the 4th century P.A., and the city was sacked by a horde of orcs and trolls who still live in the area. Whispers say that a dracolich now makes his lair in the ruins.

Entrance Cave: This is where the party entered the Upperworld. The myconid village and goblins caves are nearby.

Eryndlyn: The Domed City, a medium-sized drow city in the Middleworld. The city is built on three plateaus in a large limestone cavern with a domed ceiling.

The Fardrimm: This region is full of highways built by dwarves sometime before the Awakening. Travel through this area is fairly easy, though portions of the highways have been claimed by beholders, drow, orcs, and others.

Gauntlgrym: The ruins of a human city, now inhabited by many monsters. Travellers have reported encounters with aboleths, skum, and dire corbies, as well as wights and other undead.

Gauth Grottoes: This region is populated by many gauths and other beholderkin.

The Geode: Sprawling caverns run for miles here in the Lowerworld. Entire caves made of crystals are common in the area, and such a place resembles nothing so much as the interior of a massive geode. Strange alien beings are said to lurk in these tunnels.

Gracklstugh: The City of Blades, a large duergar city in the Middleworld on the shore of the Darklake. The area around Gracklstugh is known as the Deepkingdom.

Hellgate Keep: A demon king known as the Sceptered One rules over this fortress in the Upperworld. He has been amassing an army of summoned demons and demonically infused orcs (tanarukks) and has declared dominion over the surrounding area.

Khegrim: The ruins of an urdinnir (orecutter dwarf) city. The cavern containing the city collapsed when the dwarves left, so most of what remains is buried in rubble.

The Labyrinth: A huge maze of worked tunnels in the Upperworld and Middleworld. The entire area is occupied by tribes of minotaurs.

Mantol-Derith: A small, hidden trading city with merchant representatives from the drow, duergar, svirfneblin, and orogs. (Quarra led the party here, from which you booked travel across the Darklake to Gracklstugh.)

Menzoberranzan: The City of Spiders, a medium-sized drow city in the Upperworld. (Zov and Euryale have taken most of the party here.)

Nuur Throth: The Great Door, a huge earth node that can be used for travel to other areas of the world.

Ooltul: The City of Tyrants, a beholder city in the Lowerworld. Surprisingly, the beholders here do not generally accost visitors and the city maintains trade connections with the outside world.

The Tumulus: A mysterious, half-buried stone egg in the Lowerworld that measures some 2,000 feet in length. On its surface are inscribed symbols of death in many known and unknown languages, as well as sigils, glyphs, and signs that connote the same. Some have speculated that the egg may be hollow, but no entrance has ever been found.

Wildlands: All of the civilization in this area was destroyed during the Far Realm Invasion in the 2nd century P.A. The whole region is still crawling with bizarre aberrations.
