Svirfneblin

Silent and wary, the svirfneblin (or gnomes) dwell in mines and caverns throughout the world. Unlike more traditional gnomes, the svirfneblin are suspicious and serious creatures. Their holdings are hidden from the predatory races that live nearby because only caution, stealth, and cooperation with others of their kind stand between the svirfneblin and a terrible end.

Svirfneblin are master artisans, miners, and gemcutters. Their handiwork is prized throughout the world, and the boldest gnomes are welcomed as neutral merchants among many races. They make superior guides, scouts, and foragers because they often know passages and portals long lost to other races, and few can match the stealth or cunning of svirfneblin rangers watching over their own territory.

Personality: Svirfneblin are suspicious and slow to give their trust. They have little desire to meet new people, so most svirfneblin appear grim, sullen, and pessimistic to others. Anyone who takes the considerable trouble to befriend a gnome, however, usually finds him to be a loyal and unflinching comrade whose pragmatic outlook is balanced by a wry, self-deprecating wit.

Svirfneblin admire well-wrought metalwork and weapons, but they love the beauty of gemstones with a passion that seems impossible for creatures with such dour personalities. They are diligent, industrious, and tireless in the pursuit of excellence. Gnomes believe that anything worth doing is worth doing well, no matter how long it takes or how difficult it turns out to be.

Physical Description: A svirfneblin stands between 3 and 3-1/2 feet tall and weighs between 40 and 45 pounds. He is wiry and lean, with a body as hard as a slab of stone. His skin may be either mottled gray or dun-colored (a good match for the rock around his home), and his eyes are either dark gray or black. A female svirfneblin has hair the same color as her eyes, but a male is entirely bald and beardless.

Relations: Gnomes are deeply suspicious of all other races, particularly drow and kuo-toas. To a svirfneblin, a stranger is an enemy, and the best way to deal with enemies is to avoid them completely. Gnomes are most accepting of neutral or good creatures such as gloamings, since these folk rarely threaten them. But they deal very cautiously with quaggoths and orogs, having learned that members of these races are all too willing to plunder others who are too weak to defend themselves. Svirfneblin traders take care to meet with merchants of other races in neutral caverns that feature plenty of potential exits, in case a deal goes sour.

Alignment: Svirfneblin believe that their survival depends on avoiding entanglements with other races, so they strongly favor neutral alignments. While they rarely wish others ill, neither are they especially willing to take risks on behalf of others.

Religion: The svirfneblin venerate a god known as Callarduran Smoothhands, the Master of Stone.

Adventurers: While most svirfneblin have little use for adventuring, they are without a doubt the best guides, scouts, and pathfinders in the world. Quick, clever, and stealthy, svirfneblin have an uncanny knack for finding their way through the bleak maze of caverns and avoiding dangerous encounters along the way. A few svirfneblin find their way into the adventuring life by serving as guides for parties composed of other races.

Racial Traits

  • Ability Score Modifiers: Svirfneblin are fast and observant but relatively weak and emotionally distant. They gain −2 Strength, +2 Dexterity, +2 Wisdom, and −4 Charisma.
  • Type: Svirfneblin are humanoids with the gnome subtype.
  • Size: Svirfneblin are Small creatures and thus gain a +1 size bonus to their AC, a +1 size bonus on attack rolls, a −1 penalty to their CMB and CMD, and a +4 size bonus on Stealth checks.
  • Speed: Svirfneblin have a base speed of 20 feet.
  • Languages: Svirfneblin begin play speaking Gnome and Undercommon. Those with high Intelligence scores can choose from the following: Aklo, Aquan, Draconic, Dwarven, Elven, Giant, Goblin, Orc, and Terran.

Defense Racial Traits

  • Defensive Training: Svirfneblin gain a +2 dodge bonus to Armor Class.
  • Fortunate: Svirfneblin gain a +2 racial bonus on all saving throws.
  • Spell Resistance: Svirfneblin have spell resistance (SR) equal to 11 + their class levels.

Feat and Skill Racial Traits

  • Skills: Svirfneblin gain a +2 racial bonus on Stealth checks; this improves to a +4 bonus underground. They gain a +2 racial bonus on Craft (alchemy) checks and Perception checks.
  • Stonecunning: Svirfneblin gain a +2 bonus on Perception checks to notice unusual stonework, such as traps and hidden doors located in stone walls or floors. They receive a check to notice such features whenever they pass within 10 feet of them, whether or not they are actively looking.

Magical Racial Traits

  • Svirfneblin Magic: Svirfneblin add +1 to the DC of any illusion spells they cast. 
  • Spell-Like Abilities: Svirfneblin have the following spell-like abilities.

    constant—nondetection

    1/day—blindness/deafness, blur, disguise self

    A svirfneblin's caster level for his spell-like abilities is equal to his character level.

Offense Racial Traits

  • Hatred: Svirfneblin receive a +1 bonus on attack rolls against humanoid creatures of the reptilian and dwarf subtypes due to training against these hated foes.

Senses Racial Traits

  • Superior Darkvision: Svirfneblin have superior darkvision, allowing them to see perfectly in the dark up to 120 feet.
  • Low-Light Vision: In addition to their darkvision, svirfneblin have low-light vision, allowing them to see twice as far as humans in conditions of dim light.
