Races

This is a list of the most common "civilized" races in the campaign world. You have met and talked with members of each of these races during your time in Gracklstugh.

Major Races

Drow (or elves) are the most numerous, powerful, and widespread of the world's native peoples. The majority of the elves live in city-states ruled by various noble Houses, each of which commands its own small army of fearless drow soldiers, cunning wizards, and zealous priestesses, as well as large contingents of slave soldiers, such as bugbears, ogres, and minotaurs. 

Duergar (or gray dwarves) are a grim and determined race that lead lives of neverending toil in their great foundry-cities. The gray dwarves are nearly as widespread and numerous as the drow, and the typical duergar realm is every bit as strong, cruel, and wealthy as a great city of the elves. But while most drow cities exist to exalt the high nobility of the elves, duergar cities exist only for the manufacture of wealth through unending labor.

Svirfneblin (or gnomes) are a silent and wary race that dwell in mines and caverns throughout the world. Unlike more traditional gnomes, the svirfneblin are suspicious and serious creatures. Their holdings are hidden from the predatory races that live nearby because only caution, stealth, and cooperation with others of their kind stand between the svirfneblin and a terrible end.

 

Minor Races

Dromites are a diminutive insectoid race that live in small, hidden hives throughout the world.  Dromites have hardened, chitin-encrusted skin, compound eyes like those of an insect, and two small antennae protruding from their brows. Called “bugs” by the ignorant, dromites share far more traits with other humanoids than they do with insects.

Goblins are a race of childlike creatures with a destructive and voracious nature that makes them almost universally despised. Many goblins are held as slaves by the duergar and drow, while others live in small tribes on the outskirts of civilized areas, launching raids for food and supplies.

Kobolds are weak, craven race that claim kinship to dragons. Though they are hardworking, clever, and blessed with a natural talent for mechanical devices and mining, they spend their days nursing grudges and hatreds instead of celebrating their own gifts. Many kobolds are kept as slaves by the drow, while others live in huge underground warrens mining, farming fungus, and building traps. Kobolds often attack travellers out of spite.

Kuo-Toas are theocratic fishfolk who dwell in the deep seas and lakes of the world. The clerics of Blibdoolpoolp, called whips, exercise iron control over the population. Second in status to the clerics are the monks, who are known as monitors. Kuo-toa make excellent guides, but they have a reputation for duplicity.  The kuo-toa are closely allied with the drow, and as such are not well-liked by the duergar.

Orogs are the primary race of orcs in this world. Unlike the orcs you are used to, orogs are not overly warlike and do not revel in the act of war, though they do enjoy its spoils. Orogs have mastered the art of forging armor and weapons from the strange ores found beneath the ground, and an orog rarely goes anywhere without being fully armed and armored.

Quaggoths are a race of monstrous humanoids that resemble albino cave bears. Primitive and simple, they are forced into slavery by other races—especially the drow. Free quaggoths live in clans throughout the world, forming primitive settlements and living by hunting and gathering or raising livestock.

Ratfolk are small, rodent-like humanoids, most often found in nomadic trading caravans. Much like the pack rats they resemble, ratfolk are tinkerers and hoarders by nature, and as a whole are masters of commerce, especially when it comes to acquiring and repairing mechanical or magical devices. Though some are shrewd merchants who carefully navigate the shifting alliances of black markets and bazaars, many ratfolk love their stockpiles of interesting items far more than money, and would rather trade for more such prizes to add to their hoards over mere coins.
