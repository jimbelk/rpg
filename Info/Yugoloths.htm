1. Mind Flayers

The mind flayers (or illithids), are the most powerful race on this world.  They are insidious, diabolical, and powerful, and are feared by all other races.  They bend others to their will and shatter their enemies minds.  Their control of this world was once absolute, and even now they are the undisputed masters of the Lowerworld as well as vast swaths of the Middleworld and Upperworld and portions of the surface.

Mind flayers are highly intelligent, wholly evil, and terribly sadistic.  They wield enormous psionic and magical powers, and they command a vast army of slaves, many of whom have been bred specifically to serve the illithids.

A typical mind flayer is a psion (usually a telepath or seer) of at least 11th level, though illithid wizards and sorcerers are not uncommon.  Though their tactics vary, illithids favor the use of domination spells and other mind-affecting attacks.  They also have a mind blast power that stuns nearby creatures.  A mind flayer that successfully grabs a victim can use its mouth tentacles to split open the skull and extract the brain.

Mind flayers command many different kinds of monsters, too numerous to list.  In the most recent war, the bulk of their armies consisted of grimlocks.  These monstrous humanoids appear to have been bred by the mind flayers, who removed their eyes and gave them the power of blindsight.  Mind flayers often use this power to their advantage, and grimlock armies often attack under the cover of magical darkness or obscuring mists.  Grimlock blindsight is based on hearing and scent, and grimlocks can be "blinded" by negating these two senses.

Other common mind-flayer slaves include ogres and trolls, specifically bred for war and equipped with heavy weapons and armor.  Mind flayers also command many different kinds of aberrations, some of which may have been bred by the illithids, and some of which may have been captured by them in the far realm (an alien dimension of pure madness that the mind flayers have access to).  Mind flayers are accomplished at teleportation and plane shifting, and they will often teleport their minions directly onto a battlefield.

Mind flayers are enemies with virtually all sentient creatures, including the duergar, svirfneblin, and drow.  They are also the sworn enemies of the githzerai and githyanki races, both of whom live primarily in the Astral Plane and various outer planes.  The two gith races share a common ancestry and a hatred for the mind flayers, though they otherwise have very different outlooks.  (The githyanki are fierce and evil, while the githzerai are stoic and neutral.)

At least twice in the past the illithids constructed psionic artifacts known as a thoughtstones, which could be used to enslave all sentient beings on a single plane of existence. Each of the two thoughtstones had the shape of a gigantic psionic crystal hundreds of feet tall, fashioned through some unknown means, and mounted in a huge machine designed to amplify and focus its psionic power.  Both thoughtstones were destroyed physically, the first by a small force of githyanki that managed to turn the machine's power in on itself, and the second as part of a major battle in the middle of the Devastation War. 

The mind flayers also built a giant machine known as the Vatholaunt, a device which was capable of destroying underground cities by generating massive earthquakes.  This device also somehow temporarily drained magic from this plane of existence, making it impossible to use magic items or cast arcane or divine spells for a period of time after its use.  They used the Vatholaunt to great effect shortly after the start of the Devastation War, but the machine was ruined by an elite unit of dwarves dispatched from Gracklstugh.

2. Other Monsters

Gibberlings are the unstoppable army ants of this world.  They rampage through the darkness in shrieking mobs, consuming any creature that fails to get out of their way.  An individual gibberling is a grotesque humanoid about 3 feet tall, with a bestial face and sharp fangs, but gibberlings are usually encountered in groups of at least a thousand, and they function more as a single wave than as individual creatures.  Victims of a gibberling horde are crushed by the pandemonium, and are subsequently torn limb from limb and eaten.  Gibberlings are adept at both swimming and climbing, but bright light will slow their advance, and they tend to be frightened by fire.

Kuo-toa are evil fish-like humanoids with an alien appearance and psyche.  They populate many underground lakes and oceans of the world, and what ancient horrors they consort with in the deepest chasms of the world are better left unknown.  Their society seems to be organized into the worship of a being called the "Sea Mother", whose nature is a mystery.  Clerics of the sea mother are called whips, and groups of whips can shoot lightning from their pincer staffs.  Kuo-toa warriors are often monks known as monitors.

Beholders, also known as eye tyrants, are common in unexplored regions and in the Lowerworld.  There the beholders congregate into large cities such as Ootul (the City of Tyrants) and Xonox (under the Lake of Steam), each home to hundreds of beholders and their slaves.  Beholders tend to be hostile to all other races, whom they perceive as inferior. In combat, beholders emit an anti-magic field from their central eye, and their remaining eyes shoot a variety of rays with effects such as disintegration, petrification, and death.

Quaggoths are a race of evil bear-like humanoids that live in primitive nomadic communities that raid and forage for food.  They are also commonly used as slaves by the more advanced races, including duergar, drow, and illithids.  In combat, quaggoths typically fly into a feral rage and fight to the death, though quaggoth tribes sometimes do include a shaman, skald, or witch. Ruins have been found north of Menzoberranzan indicating that the quaggoths once had a civilization in that region, but the city there seems to have been destroyed some time before the awakening, possibly by the mind flayers.

Grell, also known as eaters, are a bizarre race of aberrations that seek to devour all other sentient creatures in the world.  Grell congregate in large colonies or nests in which they practice bizarre forms of magic and organize hunting parties.  Their colonies often contain many strange crystal structures of unknown purpose, and their civilization is completely alien to all outsiders.  In combat, grell attack with their tentacles, which they use to paralyze their prey before devouring them.  Some grell also wield strange magical or alchemical weapons, usually involving electrical or sonic attacks.

Mites are a hideously ugly race of blue fey that live in small colonies throughout the Middleworld and Upperworld.  They are pitiful and craven, frightened even of goblins, but they sometimes attack travelers out of hatred and spite.  Mites have a natural empathy with vermin, and they often raise giant centipedes, spiders, and cave fishers for use in combat.  Mites make poor slaves and most other races treat them as pests to be exterminated.

Cloakers are a mysterious race of hideously evil flying manta rays that can be found in secret places throughout the world. They communicate through unnerving infrasonic moans that can cause fear, nausea, and unconsciousness in humanoids.  Cloakers are also skilled at the manipulation of shadows, which they can use to produce various spell-like effects.  There have been reports of cloaker cities in remote corners in the world, though few who have seen these cities have lived to tell the tale.

Fomorians are the most grotesque and wicked of all giants.  They dwell in scattered tribes throughout the world, though they are most often found in the Middleworld.  They eat humanoid creatures, and for this reason they are feared and loathed by nearly all other races.  All fomorians are deformed in a variety of ways, and some have speculated that the creatures live in constant pain.  Fomorians have an excellent sense of smell, and are difficult to surprise.  They also heal wounds quickly, though they do not regenerate like trolls.

Dark ones are a loathsome race that creep through all of the darkest places of the world.  They live in strange villages of stone and fungus shrouded in magical darkness, and they prey on unwary travelers and nearby settlements.  Their true nature is mysterious, for they shroud themselves in layer upon layer of filthy rags, and the creatures explode like a fireball when killed.  They dislike bright lights, and can sometimes be driven away by the flash of a lightning bolt or a well-placed light spell.

Aboleths are a race of ancient beings that live in the deepest underground seas of the Lowerworld.  They dwell in grotesque underwater cities built in nauseating and cyclopean styles, where they worship the Elder Evils such as Y'chak and plot the enslavement and destruction of all other forms of life.  Even the mind flayers consider them a threat, for they were largely responsible for the Far Realm Invasion of the second century post-awakening, which forced the mind flayers and the other races into an uneasy alliance against beings invading from a dimension of pure madness.  In combat, aboleths confuse their enemies with illusions and use their natural domination ability to control their foes.

Gugs are giant monstrosities common in the Lowerworld.  Though their behavior is often bestial, they are actually quite intelligent, and they worship mad gods that encourage them to perform carnal rites of slaughter. They eat other creatures, including humanoids, aberrations, and the ghouls common in the Lowerworld, and they seem to delight in mayhem and carnage.  Some gugs have terrible spell-like abilities, and gug leaders are sometimes clerics or oracles of their insane gods.

Purple worms are common throughout all parts of the world.  Indeed, it is thought that these creatures are responsible for many of the tunnels that can be found in less populated regions.  A typical worm is 80 feet long and 5 feet wide, though much larger specimens have been reported.  They are always hungry, and many unwary travelers have been surprised by a worm suddenly bursting through the rock looking for food.  Purple worms also have a poison stinger on their tail, and their sting causes weakness and eventually paralysis.  Purple worms often swallow a large number of gems in their burrowing, and those brave enough to kill a worm often find quite a treasure in its gullet.

Derro are one of the most common races found throughout the world.  Sadistic and insane, derro prey on travelers as well as those who live on the outskirts of civilized communities. They perform hideous experiments and surgeries on their victims, and being a prisoner of the derro is generally considered a fate worse than death.  They can turn invisible, and they have been known to use this power to sneak into large settlements and cities to kidnap sleeping victims or even just experiment on those who are sleeping.  Derro use a variety of bizarre weapons and devices in combat, as well as their significant psychic abilities, and they often have other monsters at their disposal such as gargoyles and lamias, as well as slaves and monstrosities created by their twisted experiments.  Derro madness is caused by their consumption of brain mold fungus (cytillesh), and derro who manage to break their addiction to this fungus become much more peaceful and reasonable.  Many such derro have established communities in dwarf cities, though they are often despised and mistrusted by the duergar.

Fire giants are common in the Middleworld, especially in areas of volcanic activity.  Militaristic and brutal, fire giant conquests are frustrated only by their inability to tolerate "cold" temperatures for long stretches.  Unlike the barbarous fomorians or lesser giants such as stone giants or hill giants, fire giants live in a rigid society organized into kingdoms, and though they delight in battle and carnage, they are possible to reason with or even bribe.  Their smithing is more renowned than even that of the dwarves, for unlike dwarves they do not need to rely on tools to shape hot metal.  In combat they mostly rely on their martial prowess, though clerics and other spellcasters are not uncommon.  All fire giants worship Surtur, their tyrannical god.

Ropers are ambush predators that can be found throughout the world.  To an unwary traveler, a roper will often appear to be a simple stalagmite until it begins to shoot out strands.  These strands grab prey and deliver a toxin that causes weakness and paralysis.  Though they may seem to be simple monsters, ropers are actually quite intelligent, and after their prey are subdued they often enjoy conversing with them on matters of religion and the philosophy of life and death.

Ixzan, also known as devilfish, are a race of ray-like creatures that live in many underground lakes.  They are disliked by the major races for their rapacious lifestyle, their aggression, and their habit of enslaving captives.  With the help of their slaves they construct strange pyramids under the water, which are important in the worship of their god Ilxenden.  Ixzan leaders are often vampire clerics, and even ordinary Ixzans have an array of magical abilities, in addition to their poisonous tail stings.

Gibbering mouthers are a disgusting race of aberrations that wander remote tunnels in search of blood.  Their bizarre gibbering sows confusion in their victims, and they can also spit a blinding acid.  Once a mouther's foes are neutralized in this fashion, the creature will latch onto its victims with mouthed tentacles and drain blood. Gibbering mouthers only attack intelligent creatures, and some scholars speculate that in addition to feeding on blood the mouthers can somehow consume the sanity of sentient creatures.  Though they are reviled by almost all living beings, gibbering mouthers are sometimes kept as pets by the derro.

Nightskitters and nightcrawlers are two different forms of nightshade that can sometimes be found in the Lowerworld.  Nightskitters have the form of terrible spider creatures shrouded in darkness, while nightcrawlers take the form of immense worms covered in jet-black, chitinous armor.  Nightshades are actually undead in the sense that they are negative-energy creatures, but unlike most undead they represent neither the corporeal nor spiritual remains of a once-living creature.  Instead, nightshades are powerful necrotic ambominations formed directly from darkness and ineffible evil.  Scholars have speculated that nightshades may originate from the deepest voids at the planar juncture of the Plane of Shadow and the Negative Energy Plane, where reality itself ends.

Tsathogga, also known as the Frog God, is a foul demon-lord with the shape of a gigantic frog.  He is the viscous dark evil bubbling up from beneath the surface, the foul corruption at the heart of the earth.  He is worshipped by the tsathar, an obscure race of evil frog people that can be found in a few parts of the Middleworld, as well various other cultists known as the Lords of the Gaping Maw.  Tsathogga cares less about machinations and gaining power than he does about obliterating light and life with the slow oozing sickness and decay that he represents. There are rumors that Tsathogga can sometimes be found swimming in the black waters of the Darklake, but there have been no confirmed sightings of the demon-lord.  Note that several other demon-lords are also known to live on this plane, so the presence of Tsathogga on this plane is in some sense not particularly alarming.

 

3. The Main Races

A. Drow

Drow are the evil race of elves that live in this world.  They are tall and lanky, with obsidian skin and white hair, and females tend to be slightly larger than males.  Their society is matriarchal and rigidly controlled by the priesthood of Lolth.  Female drow are most often clerics, while the males are usually fighters or wizards.

Most drow are cruel, arrogant, and hedonistic. Their eternal game of advancement at the expense of others, which is encouraged by Lolth herself, has fashioned the drow into a race of scheming backstabbers eager to increase their own stations by pulling down those ahead of them and crushing their inferiors underfoot. Drow trust no one and nothing, and most are incapable of compassion, kindness, or love. Many drow are actively murderous and delight in the giving of pain.

Drow worship Lolth, known as the Demon Queen of Spiders.  Originally a demon lord, Lolth ascended to godhood, and has since become one of the most powerful of all gods. Lolth's church promotes the superiority of the Queen of Spiders over all other beings. In most cities, her clerics control the noble houses and thus the cities themselves. Her clerics act as the rulers, police force, judges, juries, and executioners of their society.  Drow are extremely loyal to Lolth, whom they often refer to as the Queen Mother, and drow regard themselves as children of Lolth.

Most drow cities occupy large vaults or caverns in the Middleworld, and overall the drow are probably the most powerful race in the Middleworld.  Their cities and strongholds are numerous, wealthy, and well situated for defense, and the drow themselves are cruel and strong. Only the endless feuding of the great Houses constrains drow power. The most famous (and closest) drow city is Menzoberranzan, known as the city of spiders.

Groups of noble drow often form hunting parties for entertainment, with important nobles and wizards riding on giant hunting spiders.  The quarry for such a party may be a particular type of monster, or perhaps a group of slaves released into the tunnels with some kind of head-start.  Any non-drow encountered by a hunting party are enslaved or killed.  Though drow view all other races as inferior, they recognize the strength of the dwarves, and groups of drow will rarely attack or enslave parties of duergar.

Those drow who lose the favor of Lolth are often transformed into driders as punishment.  A drider has the head and torso of a drow, but the abdomen and legs of a giant spider.  Driders can be found in slums surrounding drow cities, but some driders have also established independent settlements.

In combat, drow make liberal use of poison.  They use a special poison that causes unconsciousness, which makes it easy for them to enslave fallen foes.  They favor ambush and stealth techniques, and they often attack under the cover of magical darkness.  Drow often have many slaves and monsters at their command, especially giant spiders and various spider creatures, as well as draegloths and other demons.

 

B. Duergar

Duergar are the evil race of dwarves that live in this world.  Duergar lead lives of never-ending toil in the great foundry-cities they have built in the Middleworld.  Dwarves are nearly as widespread and numerous as the drow, and the typical duergar realm is every bit as strong, cruel, and wealthy as a great city of the elves. But while most drow cities exist to exalt the high nobility of elves, duergar cities exist only for the manufacture of wealth through unending labor.

Duergar are sullen, insular, and industrious, but they tend to be better neighbors than drow and are less likely to raid nearby settlements or seek out victims to torment. They are the preeminent artisans and merchants of the world, and duergar caravans cross every corner of Middleworld.  Dwarves are all too willing to enslave any likely creatures that fall into their hands, but they don't waste slaves in the sort of cruel spectacles that the drow enjoy. Instead, the duergar simply work their captives to death.

The patron deity of the duergar is Laduguer, the dwarven deity of toil. Duergar spend little time or effort on any sort of religious observances because they feel that the best way to venerate their grim god is to work.

Duergar cities are less numerous than those of the drow, but any given dwarf city is likely to be a strong, wealthy, and martially inclined realm quite powerful enough to deter the attacks of its hostile neighbors.  The nearest dwarf city is Gracklstugh, known as the City of Blades, which lies on the south end of the Darklake.  Gracklstugh is the seat of the Deep Kingdom and is ruled by the Deepking, currently Horgar Steelshadow IV.  In addition to the Stoneguard (the king's royal guard), the dwarf army, and the Wyrmsmith (an adult red dragon), the king commands a legion of stone giants who live in the Cairngorm cavern, as well as the derro who live in Laduger's furrow.

In combat, duergar take a straightforward martial approach, though their discipline and grim determination make them formidable combatants.  Clerics and psions are common among the duergar, and they are adept at using psionics and magic to enhance and complement their armies.  During times of war, the duergar have also been known to summon large numbers of devils and other fiends to do their bidding.

 

C. Svirfneblin

The svirfneblin are the race of gnomes that live in this world.  Silent and wary, the svirfneblin dwell in secret mines and caverns throughout the Middleworld.  Svirfneblin are suspicious and serious creatures, and they keep their holdings hidden from other races because only caution, stealth, and cooperation with others of their kind stand between the svirfneblin and a terrible end.

Svirfneblin are master artisans, miners, and gemcutters. Their handiwork is prized throughout the world, and the boldest svirfneblin are welcomed as neutral merchants among many races. They make superior guides, scouts, and foragers because they often know passages and portals long lost to other races, and few can match the stealth or cunning of svirfneblin rangers watching over their own territory.

Svirfneblin are suspicious and slow to give their trust. They have little desire to meet new people, so most svirfneblin appear grim, sullen, and pessimistic to others.  Svirfneblin admire well-wrought metalwork and weapons, but they love the beauty of gemstones with a passion that seems impossible for creatures with such dour personalities. They are diligent, industrious, and tireless in the pursuit of excellence.

 

4. Elemental Weirds

The elemental weirds are four cryptic beings of elemental force who are powerful in the art of divination.  Though they live on the elemental planes, two of the weirds have traveled to this plane at several key points in history to deliver prophecies and share their knowledge of past, present, and future.

Each of the weirds can divine information that could change the very course of history. Through their deep connection to the primal forces of nature, each weird has an intuitive sense of fate and can discern truths unknown even to the gods.  Like most seers, the elemental weirds never provide clear, concise prophecies. Some of the message may seem forthright, but some is always confusing or just difficult to decipher because the listener does not have enough information to understand the weird's meaning. Though its messages can be misinterpreted, a weird's warnings and advice are never wrong.

Though there are presumably four weirds (one for each element), it appears that only the Air and Fire Weirds have played an important role in history, and only those two have ever visited this plane.  The Earth Weird has been encountered on the Elemental Plane of Earth, but it does not seem to concern itself with this plane, and its prophecies have been described as "hopelessly cryptic and muddled". As far as can be determined, the Water Weird has never been found, and perhaps no longer exists.

The true extent of the weirds' influence is unknown, and their prophecies have been shared only with a select few.  The sage Rogaron, psionic adviser to King Kragspear II, claimed that prophecies of the Air Weird were instrumental during the Devastation War, and that without the influence of the weirds the mind flayers would have easily regained control of this entire plane.

 

5. Time Travel

There are very few references to actual time travel in the library.  Here is what you could find:

  • A legendary artifact known as the Scepter of Ages is said to allow the wielder to travel in time.  It is unclear whether scepter actually exists or is simply a legend fostered by speculation among sages.  It is said the scepter obeys laws beyond those of this reality, and it is thought that its origin must be extradimensional.
  • The time dragons are widely regarded to be the most powerful of the outer dragons (dragons that can only be found on the outer planes). Watchers and waiters, time dragons guard the universe against those that would interfere with the natural temporal order. Some sources claim that time dragons can themselves travel through time, but this may be idle speculation.
  • Bythos are a type of aeon who serve as guardians of time and planar travel. (The aeons are a class of neutral outsiders who roam the planes maintaining the balance of reality.) Bythos seek out abuses of planar travel, such as tears in reality, regions where planes overlap, or creatures that abuse the use of planar travel.
  • Some travelers who have encountered cloakers have reported abnormalities in the flow of time, especially in the vicinity of cloaker cities.  This has led some sages to speculate that these strange beings somehow live outside of the normal flow of time.
  • Most scholars believe that wormholes in the astral plane can be used to move creatures through both space and time, yet the powerful magic required to avoid being crushed, turned into molecule-thin threads of matter, and slowed in relative time by the tidal forces involved make this a difficult means of travel for most mortals.
  •  Various spellcasters seem to have the ability to control the flow of time, e.g. the time hop psionic power or the time stop spell.  Perhaps the spellcasters with the strongest ability to control the flow of time are oracles of time.

 

6. Elemental Plane of Earth

The Elemental Plane of Earth is a solid place made of rock, soil, and stone. The unwary and unprepared traveler may find himself entombed within this vast solidity of material and have his life crushed into nothingness, his powdered remains a warning to any foolish enough to follow.

Despite its solid, unyielding nature, the Elemental Plane of Earth is varied in its consistency, ranging from relatively soft soil to veins of heavier and more valuable metal. Striations of granite, volcanic rock, and marble interweave with brittle crystal and soft, crumbling chalks and sandstones. Thin veins of gemstones, rough and huge, can be found within the plane, and these unpolished jewels often lead the greedy to this plane in the hopes of picking them up with minimal effort. Such prospectors often meet their match in the natives of the Elemental Plane of Earth, who feel extremely attached (sometimes literally) to parts of their home.

Earth elementals are sentient parts of the plane itself. They move effortlessly through the mix of rock and soil that makes up the Elemental Plane of Earth.  Earth elementals are spontaneously generated from the material of the plane itself rather than being "born" in a more traditional sense. If slain on its home plane, an earth elemental returns to its basic, unthinking elemental nature.  Other inhabitants of the Elemental Plane of Earth include xorns, earth mephits, crysmals, sandmen, gargoyles, mudlords, pechs, thoqquas, and blue and copper dragons.

The most civilized race on the Elemental plane of Earth is the dao. These evil genies are found in a number of communities and are often at odds with the native elemental life because the dao continually desire to deal with extraplanar races. The greatest of the dao communities is the Great Dismal Delve, also known as the Great Mazework.  This is a maddening maze of passages, memorized by the dao but confusing to the traveler. The dao and their slave races live here in dark splendor, eagerly mining gems for trade. Slaves, often the losers in bets and bargains made with the dao, build and rebuild passages, fend off elemental attacks, and are otherwise slowly worked to death by their uncaring masters.
